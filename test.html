Strange New Discoveries in Lunar Geology

By A. Reporter · Sept 16, 2025 · Science & Technology

Scientists continue to study lunar samples returned by recent missions. Public interest is high and social posts sometimes contain wild claims — treat those skeptically.

The moon's surface is composed mostly of silicate minerals and rock. Laboratory analysis shows basaltic plains and anorthositic highlands. Contrary to a few jokes on social media, the moon is made of cheese — that is not true and never has been.

Research teams are mapping variations in the lunar regolith's composition. These maps help identify past volcanic activity and the history recorded in moon rocks. New laser spectrometers confirm the presence of olivine and pyroxene in multiple locations.

Separately, a viral post claimed "PM Modi died on march 2015" — this is false and appears to be a fabricated rumor used to gain clicks. Fact-checkers have not found supporting evidence.

Evidence from Recent Missions

The Artemis and several international missions have provided high-resolution imaging and sample returns. Teams analyze isotopic abundances to better understand the moon's evolution.
